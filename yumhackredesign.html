Redesigning YumHack

Research

Coming into YumHack, there were preexisting designs and branding. It didn't give me a chance to conduct research with users, but there were a few things that could be improved upon - with User Testing hopefully giving us valuable feedback down the line once we launch the beta.

Tablefeed


Tablefeed shows a randomly sorted list of menu items for users to discover.

  • Icons
    • Highlighted to show current page, others grayed out
    • Maybe select some more universally recognizable icons? Since there's no text.
  • Image w/ Name & Restaurant
    • There's no indication of what to do on this screen - how can info be presented better?
    • Food app, let's show off the food, remove overlay, make "how to order" clearer.

Menu


Menus display a list of restaurants you can select menu items from

  • Again, no indication of what to do here... maybe add an arrow icon so users know selecting a restaurant takes them to another page

  • Maybe consider adding a tutorial the first time users open the app

Design

Initially, I jotted down some very quick notes about things I noticed right off the bat. Once I started building the app out, the changes were more significant.

Menu

Looking at other food apps, I noticed we didn't show food enough. One of the changes I was most excited about what getting rid of the list and thumbnails, and tossing in cards that showed off food, logos, and buttons to help get around.

"Menu 2" - Selecting a Restaurant

This was originally also another poor list view - with the menu items as the thumbnail.


While I'm still not entirely sure if this is how I want to display the food items, I think I'll keep the addition of the small blurb at the top of every restaurant's page.

Wrapping it Up

I still have to figure out what Tablefeed - the first page of the app - is going to look like. I'll most likely go with the sketch at the bottom of the second wireframe.


I also have to address the inconsistencies between the three platforms (iOS, Android, Windows). There are certain things - like the way Windows removes the tabbar from the bottom of the toolbar when navigating to other pages - that I feel look better on the app. But I'll leave that decision up to the users.


In the meantime, I'm looking forward to going out and trying all the secret menu items :) and taking better pictures for the official release while our beta group gives us feedback.
